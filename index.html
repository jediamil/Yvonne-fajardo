Portfolio Rationale

This e-portfolio serves as a comprehensive reflection of my learning journey in 2S-ECED13: Physical Education, Music, Arts, Drama for Children (PEMAD). It showcases my growing understanding of these disciplines and demonstrates how they can be effectively integrated into early childhood education.

Through carefully curated artifacts and reflections, this portfolio documents my progress, challenges overcome, and key insights gained throughout the course. It ultimately highlights my commitment to fostering creative development and lifelong learning in young children.

Course Description

Welcome to 2S-ECED13: PEMAD

This course explores the transformative power of arts integration in early childhood development, focusing on:

  • Physical-motor development through movement and coordination activities
  • Socio-emotional growth via collaborative creative expression
  • Cognitive skill-building through arts-based learning approaches

Students engage with fundamental processes through appreciation, performance, and creation, developing practical skills for implementing arts integration in educational settings.

Workshop 2: Physical Education - Grade 3

Lesson Integration: Matematinik (Math - Multiplication)

Focus Area Details
FMS (Fundamental Motor Skills) Jumping, Hopping, Running
Activity Title Matematinik: Multiplication Game with Movement
Objective Integrate multiplication with physical activity using the Filipino game “10-20” in a relay-style classroom game.
Mechanics
  • Students are divided into two groups and lined up.
  • Each student draws a number (1–10) from their group’s draw lot set.
  • Paired students from both lines calculate the multiplication (e.g., 9 x 5).
  • The front students jump toward the correct answer position.
  • Once completed, they tag the next player to continue the relay.
Materials
  • Garter
  • 2 sets of draw lots (numbers 1–10)

Workshop 2: Music - Grade 2

Lesson Integration: Language and Vocabulary Development

Focus Area Details
FMS (Fine Motor Skills) Grip and Pencil Control, Hand-Eye Coordination, Spatial Awareness
Activity Title Tune In and Sort It Out!
Objective Identify and classify different types of musical instruments to support vocabulary development and observation skills.
Mechanics
  • The teacher introduces musical instrument categories: string, wind, percussion, and keyboard.
  • Students receive worksheets with various instrument pictures.
  • They observe and label the type of each instrument.
  • The class reviews and discusses the answers together.
Materials Worksheets with instrument images, pencils/pens, visual aids or posters
Target Learners Grade 2 Students

Workshop 2: Arts - Grade 1

Lesson Integration: Shapes, Design & Storytelling

Focus Area Details
FMS (Fine Motor Skills) Hand-Eye Coordination, Finger Grip, Finger Dexterity, Problem Solving, Cognitive Flexibility
Activity Title Tangram Creative Construction
Objective Promote creativity, spatial awareness, and storytelling through shape recognition and tangram figure construction.
Mechanics
  • Each group receives a tangram set.
  • Students use the pieces to build a figure of their choice.
  • They present their figure and share a story related to it with the class.
  • Focus on following instructions and engaging in creative thinking.
Subjects Integrated
  • Mathematics: Shapes
  • Arts: Patterns and Design, Color Recognition
  • Language Arts: Storytelling, Following Instructions

Workshop Gallery: Arts - Grade 1

Workshop 1 & 2: Drama - All Levels

Lesson Integration by Level (Pre-K to Grade 3)

Level FMS & Skills Activity Mechanics
Pre-Kinder Imagination, Gross Motor Skills, Creativity Magic Hat Adventure
  • One child wears an imaginary "magic hat."
  • They pretend to become a character (e.g., astronaut).
  • Each student takes turns imagining and acting out a new role.
Kinder Bending, Stretching, Turning, Cause and Effect, Improvisation How Will You Act, How Will You React?
  • Pairs choose an emotion (joy, anger, sadness, etc.).
  • One acts out the emotion, the other reacts.
  • They switch roles and repeat.
Grade 1 Twisting, Turning, Stretching, Stopping, Action Words Act and Freeze
  • Teacher plays music and says an action (e.g. wash the dishes).
  • Students act it out while dancing.
  • When the teacher shouts "freeze!", they hold their pose.
  • Points awarded for accuracy and creativity.
Grade 2 Improvisation, Problem Solving, Physical Expression Mystery Item Role Play
  • Groups pick a random object and scenario slip.
  • Create a story that includes the object in a creative way.
  • Act out their short scene for the class.
Grade 3 Walking, Running, Carrying, Bending, Turning, Cause and Effect Reverse Drama
  • Groups receive a story ending and work backward to create the beginning.
  • Perform a 2–3 minute skit showing what caused the ending.
  • Use role-playing, props, and movement.

Course Reflection

Looking back on my PEMAD journey — the lessons, challenges, and growth that shaped me as an educator

A Journey of Enrichment

My journey through 2S-ECED13: PEMAD has been an enriching experience that expanded my understanding of the impact of Physical Education, Music, Arts, and Drama on young learners. I was able to design engaging lesson plans, games, and meaningful activities that nurture holistic development.

The Power of Integration

🏃

Physical Education

Builds motor skills, coordination, and healthy habits through movement and play.

🎵

Music

Enhances creativity, rhythm awareness, and emotional expression through sound.

🎨

Arts

Opens doors to imagination, visual storytelling, and creative problem-solving.

🎭

Drama

Fosters empathy, communication skills, and confidence through role-play.

Unexpected Challenges

A memorable challenge was during our Drama Workshop, when an activity didn't go as planned. My teammate and I had to improvise a new game for pre-K students on the spot. It taught me the value of adaptability and spontaneity in teaching — skills I now consider essential for any educator.

Transformative Takeaways

1

Deep Appreciation for Creative Learning

Seeing firsthand how arts integration sparks joy and engagement in young learners

2

Practical Activity Design Skills

Developing a toolkit of adaptable, developmentally-appropriate activities

3

Confident, Flexible Teaching

Learning to trust my instincts and adapt to the moment's needs

"The most engaging activities often arise from spontaneous improvisation and a genuine desire to connect with children — this course helped me find both the confidence and creativity to make those moments happen."

I'm excited to carry these experiences into my future classroom, where I can nurture joyful, creative, and meaningful learning journeys for every child I meet.
